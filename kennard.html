History of Kennard

Kennard was officially platted on September 6, 1882, by surveyor Daniel K. Cook in Greensboro Township, Henry County, Indiana. The town was laid out in 12 blocks containing 56 lots and named after Jenkins Kennard. Cyrus C. Hinshaw, a key founder and the town’s first postmaster (appointed September 12, 1882), played a major role in its establishment. Hinshaw Street in Kennard is named in honor of him and his family.

For its first century, Kennard served as a small agricultural service center for surrounding farms, with a post office, general stores, and a sawmill. The town celebrated its centennial in 1982 with a two-day event.

Sources: Henry County Historical Society records and Discover Indiana History (Indiana Historical Bureau).

Kennard Elementary School & the 1974 Tornado

On April 3, 1974, during the historic “Super Outbreak” (one of the largest tornado outbreaks in U.S. history), an F4 tornado struck Kennard. The tornado destroyed or severely damaged more than 70% of the town, leveling 48 homes and completely destroying the nearly 100-year-old Kennard Elementary School. Seventeen people were injured. A four-day-old premature baby, born to an injured mother, sadly passed away four days later.

Principal Don Newcomer became a hometown hero: just days earlier he had conducted a tornado drill, and when the storm hit he ran from classroom to classroom directing 175 students and staff to the safe basement area. No children were seriously injured.

After the tornado, the Kennard Elementary School was never rebuilt. Students were consolidated into the Charles A. Beard Memorial School Corporation, which remains the primary district today.

War & Community History

Kennard, like much of Henry County, has deep Quaker roots and a strong Union sentiment during the Civil War. No specific large-scale war memorials or famous residents from Kennard are documented in major historical records, but the town contributed sons to every major U.S. conflict. The Henry County Historical Society preserves many local veterans’ stories from the area.

Recent & Current Notes

Kennard remains a quiet, close-knit community focused on preserving its history and small-town character. Recent town council meetings and community projects continue to focus on infrastructure and quality of life for its approximately 450 residents.
