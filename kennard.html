History of Kennard

Kennard was officially platted on September 6, 1882, by surveyor Daniel K. Cook in Greensboro Township, Henry County, Indiana. The town was named after Jenkins Kennard. Cyrus C. Hinshaw, the town’s first postmaster (appointed September 12, 1882), played a major role in its founding. Hinshaw Street in Kennard is named in his honor.

For its first century, Kennard served as a small agricultural service center for surrounding farms, with a post office, general stores, and a sawmill. The town celebrated its centennial in 1982.

Sources: Henry County Historical Society and Discover Indiana History (Indiana Historical Bureau).

Kennard School History & the 1974 Tornado

Prior to 1974, Kennard operated its own K-12 school. On April 3, 1974, during the historic “Super Outbreak,” an F4 tornado struck Kennard. The tornado destroyed or severely damaged more than 70% of the town, leveling 48 homes and completely destroying the nearly 100-year-old Kennard K-12 school building.

After the tornado, a new elementary building was constructed north of the old site and operated as **Kennard Elementary School** until its final year in 2013. The building was sold in 2015. High school students were bussed to Knightstown immediately after the tornado.

In 2015, Charles A. Beard Memorial School Corporation closed Kennard Elementary as part of a larger centralization effort. The current Knightstown Elementary and Intermediate schools are in Knightstown and the district is seeking to build new ones to create a campus that has all buildings in one area.

Source for Kennard Elementary School (Closed 2015): PublicSchoolReview.com – Kennard Elementary School Profile

Recent History

In recent decades, Kennard has continued to grow and adapt. During the COVID-19 pandemic, the town responded quickly by increasing its medical volunteer personnel and purchasing a newer vehicle to improve emergency medical assistance. Over the past ten years, the town has developed three new parks for residents to enjoy. Most recently, Kennard has experienced a noticeable housing boom as more families choose to settle in this quiet, close-knit community.
