History of Kennard

Kennard was officially platted on September 6, 1882, by surveyor Daniel K. Cook in Greensboro Township, Henry County, Indiana. The town was named after Jenkins Kennard. Cyrus C. Hinshaw, the town’s first postmaster, played a major role in its founding. Hinshaw Street in Kennard is named in his honor.

The 1974 Tornado

On April 3, 1974, during the historic Super Outbreak, an F4 tornado struck Kennard and destroyed or severely damaged more than 70% of the town, including the nearly 100-year-old K-12 school building.

School History

After the tornado, a new elementary building was constructed and operated as Kennard Elementary School until its final year in 2013. The building was sold in 2015. High school students were bussed to Knightstown immediately after the tornado. Kennard Elementary closed in 2015 as part of the Charles A. Beard Memorial School Corporation’s centralization effort.

Recent History

In recent decades, Kennard has continued to grow. During the COVID-19 pandemic, the town increased its medical volunteer personnel and purchased a newer vehicle to improve emergency medical assistance. Over the past ten years, three new parks have been developed, and the town has experienced a noticeable housing boom.
