School District

The Town of Kennard is within the Charles A. Beard Memorial School Corporation (approximately 8 miles from Kennard to Knightstown campus).

Current Schools Serving Kennard

Kennard students attend Knightstown Elementary, Knightstown Intermediate, and Knightstown High School.

After the 1974 tornado, high school students were bussed to Knightstown. Kennard Elementary operated until its final year in 2013 and was sold in 2015 as part of centralization efforts.

School Bus Stop: Closest Knightstown bus stop for Kennard students is at the intersection of Main Street and State Road 234 (approximately 1.5 miles from central Kennard).

Kennard School History

Before consolidation, Kennard had its own high school and elementary school. The high school’s mascot was the **Leopardcats** — a fierce, spotted wildcat that represented the town’s athletic teams from at least the 1940s until 1957. The school colors were black and red.

In 1957, Kennard High School consolidated with Knightstown High School. Knightstown kept the black-and-red colors but changed the mascot to the Panthers, continuing the cat theme.

The 1974 tornado severely damaged the old Kennard Elementary building. By that time, the high school had already merged years earlier, so the Leopardcats mascot did not survive to see the tornado’s impact.
