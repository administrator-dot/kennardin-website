School District

The Town of Kennard is within the Charles A. Beard Memorial School Corporation (approximately 8 miles from Kennard to Knightstown campus).

Current Schools Serving Kennard

Kennard students attend Knightstown Elementary, Knightstown Intermediate, and Knightstown High School.

Kennard High School consolidated with Knightstown High School in 1957. By 1974 the town was served only by Kennard Elementary School (grades K-6). On April 3, 1974, an F4 tornado destroyed the original Kennard Elementary building. The school was rebuilt just north of the destroyed site and proudly continued to serve the community with distinction for nearly four decades. Kennard Elementary consistently achieved academic results above both district and state averages, earning the deep pride of many citizens and alumni who still fondly remember their time as Kennard students. Unfortunately, financial pressures within the Charles A. Beard Memorial School Corporation led to the closure of the beloved local school in 2013 as part of a larger consolidation effort.

School Bus Stop: Closest Knightstown bus stop for Kennard students is at the intersection of Main Street and State Road 234. Shirley has a bus stop for Eastern. Shenandoah has a bus stop North of town 1 mile at the Baptist church parking lot.

Memorable Local Alumni

Steve Alford

New Castle High School (Class of 1983)

Achievements: Indiana Mr. Basketball 1983 • NCAA National Champion 1987 (Indiana Hoosiers) • NBA player (Dallas Mavericks, Golden State Warriors) • Head coach at UCLA and New Mexico.

Local Importance: Led New Castle to back-to-back state championships in the mid-1980s. One of Indiana's most iconic high school basketball legends and a proud New Castle graduate who put Henry County on the national map.

Kent Benson

New Castle High School

Achievements: NBA player (Milwaukee Bucks, Detroit Pistons) • NCAA champion with Indiana in 1976 • 3-time All-Big Ten selection.

Local Importance: Another New Castle basketball star who helped bring statewide recognition to the Trojans program in the 1970s.
